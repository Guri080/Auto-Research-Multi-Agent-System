# Environmental and Social Impacts of Lithium Mining for Electric Vehicle Battery Production

## Executive Summary
Lithium mining for electric vehicle batteries has significant environmental and social impacts. Environmentally, it leads to groundwater depletion, biodiversity loss, pollution, and energy-intensive extraction processes that contribute to climate change. Lithium mining disproportionately affects local water resources, especially in arid regions, causing acute shortages and ecosystem damage. Socially, local communities often suffer from inadequate compensation, health risks, cultural disruption, and mental health challenges. To address these concerns, mitigation measures include improved extraction technologies, stronger environmental and social standards, renewable energy use, recycling, and enhanced community engagement practices aimed at fostering trust and collaboration.

## What are the key environmental impacts of lithium mining related to electric vehicle battery production?
Lithium mining primarily relies on energy-intensive extraction methods such as hard rock mining and brine evaporation, which contribute significantly to climate change through fossil fuel consumption. The traditional extraction processes cause pollution, land degradation, and potential groundwater contamination. They also lead to the loss of freshwater through evaporation and the release of harmful gases and chemical leaks. This results in adverse environmental effects, including damage to biodiversity and surrounding groundwater reserves, which are critical for local ecosystems [APM Research Lab](https://www.apmresearchlab.org/10x/lithium-mining-for-evs-sustainability), [Lithium Harvest](https://lithiumharvest.com/knowledge/lithium-extraction/environmental-impacts-of-lithium-mining-and-extraction/), [Earth.org](https://earth.org/lithium-and-cobalt-mining/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_impacts_of_lithium-ion_batteries).

## How does lithium mining affect local water resources and ecosystems?
Lithium mining imposes immense stress on local water supplies, often consuming up to 65% of a region’s water, thereby intensifying competition between mining operations, communities, and natural ecosystems. In many arid and semi-arid areas where lithium deposits are found, mining causes acute water shortages due to the massive volumes of saline underground water pumped to the surface for brine evaporation. This process depletes freshwater resources, disrupts water cycles, and destroys local ecosystems dependent on these water sources. The destruction includes significant impacts on biodiversity and habitat integrity [Lithium Harvest](https://lithiumharvest.com/knowledge/lithium-extraction/environmental-impacts-of-lithium-mining-and-extraction/), [EcoJungle](https://ecojungle.net/post/lithium-extraction-environmental-impact/), [Starline Home](https://www.starlinehome.com/top-ways-of-managing-the-impact-of-lithium-mining-on-the-environment/), [Nenpower](https://nenpower.com/blog/what-are-the-main-environmental-impacts-of-lithium-mining/).

## What social and economic effects does lithium mining have on local communities?
While state governments often regard lithium mining as an economic opportunity, local communities frequently feel marginalized, perceiving that they receive minimal compensation for the environmental degradation and social disruptions they endure. For instance, rural Indigenous communities express concerns over health risks and environmental damage that accompany mining activities, despite cautious optimism about potential economic benefits. Local communities face a complex trade-off between possible economic revival through mining jobs and infrastructure and the detrimental effects on their health, environment, and traditional livelihoods [The Nevada Independent](https://thenevadaindependent.com/article/were-just-somebody-little-rural-indigenous-communities-on-the-frontlines-of-energy-transition-amid-plans-to-mine-major-lithium-deposit), [Friends of the Earth Scotland](https://foe.scot/whats-the-problem-with-lithium/), [Inside Climate News](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/26082023/salton-sea-lithium-mining-california/), [Mother Jones](https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2024/01/oil-companies-lithium-mining-rural-communities/).

## What measures are being implemented to mitigate negative environmental and social impacts of lithium mining?
Current mitigation efforts include the development and adoption of direct lithium extraction (DLE) technologies, which reduce water usage and environmental footprint compared to traditional brine evaporation methods. Additionally, stronger environmental and social standards are being promoted alongside transition to renewable energy sources for mining operations and scaling up battery recycling initiatives to limit demand for virgin lithium. Sustainable mining practices are complemented by innovation and increased investment focused on environmental, social, and governance (ESG) criteria. Some geopolitical regions, such as the European Union, advocate for reducing raw material demand and greater government involvement to ensure a just energy transition [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2214790X24000960), [Renewables Energy Sources](https://renewablesenergysources.com/lithium-mining-impacts-environmental-and-social-costs-of-powering-the-battery-age/), [Farmonaut](https://farmonaut.com/mining/how-to-minimize-environmental-impact-in-deep-brine-lithium).

## What specific social impacts, such as displacement, cultural changes, or health issues, are experienced by local communities affected by lithium mining?
Local communities, especially Indigenous peoples in lithium-rich areas such as Argentina, Chile, and Peru, face severe water scarcity directly attributable to mining operations, which drastically disrupts their livelihoods and traditional practices. Occupational and environmental exposure to mining-related metals presents significant health risks, including increased incidence of mental health issues among residents living in mining zones. Lithium mining activities also impact cultural resources and sacred sites, eroding cultural heritage and community identity. These social impacts compound the environmental challenges faced by these populations, highlighting the multifaceted hardship linked to lithium extraction [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2214790X24000765), [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11348589/), [eScholarship](https://escholarship.org/content/qt4xs577dk/qt4xs577dk.pdf), [NRDC](https://www.nrdc.org/stories/lithium-mining-leaving-chiles-indigenous-communities-high-and-dry-literally).

## What targeted social mitigation strategies or community engagement practices are currently being implemented to address the negative impacts of lithium mining on local populations?
Mitigation strategies emphasize community engagement approaches that build trust and foster collaboration with local stakeholders, including residents, businesses, schools, and community organizations. Techniques such as Asset-Based Community Development and Human-Centered Design promote shared goals and ownership of mitigation efforts. Programs focus on providing technical assistance, training, and peer learning to help communities develop creative responses to mining-related challenges. Further, community engagement promotes health considerations in land reuse decisions through partnerships, communication, and education, all aimed at fostering a local culture shift that prioritizes sustainable and inclusive development [PHI](https://www.phi.org/thought-leadership/report-toolkit-strategies-for-strengthening-community-engagement/), [Creativize](https://creativize.net/community-engagement-best-practices/), [Ithy](https://ithy.com/article/community-hazard-mitigation-strategies-5kyu3ej8), [CDC](https://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/pha-guidance/engaging_the_community/community_engagement_tools_actions.html).

## Limitations
This report aggregates high-confidence information on the environmental and social impacts of lithium mining but has limited coverage on some social mitigation practices due to medium confidence and sparse sources. While multiple high-confidence facts document water and health impacts, detailed socioeconomic analyses and longitudinal studies on community responses are limited. Furthermore, the report does not cover the full life cycle of lithium use beyond mining, nor does it address impacts related to other battery materials, which may also be relevant for a comprehensive understanding of battery production impacts. Readers should independently verify localized impacts and efficacy of mitigation strategies due to regional variability.
